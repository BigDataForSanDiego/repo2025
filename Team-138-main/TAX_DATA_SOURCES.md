# San Diego County Tax & Budget Data Sources

## Official Government Sources

### San Diego County
1. **County Auditor & Controller**
   - URL: https://www.sandiegocounty.gov/auditor/
   - Contains: Budget documents, financial reports, transparency data
   - Best for: Overall county budget breakdown

2. **County Budget Documents**
   - URL: https://www.sandiegocounty.gov/content/sdc/auditor/budget.html
   - Contains: Adopted budgets, financial statements, budget summaries
   - Best for: Detailed budget allocations by department

3. **County Transparency Portal**
   - URL: https://www.sandiegocounty.gov/content/sdc/auditor/transparency.html
   - Contains: Public financial data, expenditure reports
   - Best for: Real-time spending data

4. **Health & Human Services - Homeless Services**
   - URL: https://www.sandiegocounty.gov/content/sdc/hhsa/programs/ssp/homeless_services.html
   - Contains: Homeless services funding and programs
   - Best for: Housing and homeless services budget data

### City of San Diego
5. **City Budget Office**
   - URL: https://www.sandiego.gov/finance/budget
   - Contains: City budget documents, financial reports
   - Best for: City-level budget data

## California State Sources

6. **California Department of Tax and Fee Administration (CDTFA)**
   - URL: https://cdtfa.ca.gov/dataportal/
   - Contains: State tax data, revenue collections
   - Best for: State-level tax information

## Key Budget Categories to Look For

When accessing these sources, look for:
- **Public Safety** (Sheriff, Fire, Emergency Services)
- **Health & Human Services** (includes homeless services)
- **Housing & Community Development**
- **Infrastructure** (Roads, Utilities)
- **Parks & Recreation**
- **Education**
- **Administration**

## Fiscal Year Information

- San Diego County fiscal year: **July 1 - June 30**
- Budget documents typically published: **May/June** (for upcoming fiscal year)
- Example: FY 2024-2025 runs from July 1, 2024 to June 30, 2025

## How to Extract Data

1. Download the "Adopted Budget" PDF for the fiscal year
2. Look for the "Budget Summary" or "Revenue and Expenditure Summary"
3. Find department-level allocations
4. Look specifically for:
   - Health & Human Services Agency (HHSA)
   - Housing & Community Development Services
   - Homeless Services Division

## Sample Data Structure

When you find the data, you'll typically see:
- Total Budget: $X billion
- By Department/Function
- By Fund Type (General Fund, Special Funds, etc.)
- Capital vs. Operating Expenses

## Integration Tips

- Budget documents are usually PDFs - you may need to extract tables
- Some counties provide Excel/CSV downloads
- Look for "Budget at a Glance" summaries for quick overviews
- Check for "Performance Measures" sections that show outcomes
